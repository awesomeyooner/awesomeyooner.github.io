The risks to privacy from collecting and storing personal information

When collecting and storing personal information, there are always risks for people to steal and sell your information. Hackers can steal your information and pretend to be you. When storing your personal information on someone else’s computer, ie Google Drive, there is always a risk that someone will steal your information because you aren’t in control of their property. When collecting personal information online, there might be people who are on the WiFi and can see the data being sent. Businesses might get a data breach and have a lot of sensitive information shared.

Furthermore, companies might lie about what they use with that information. A company might say that they will not share the information or see it, though some might lie and sell it to other companies to give you advertisements.

Although companies invest in defending their storage and try their best to ensure that none of the data gets breached, there is always a chance that it might happen. Source: link

Computing Innovation can be beneficial by improving the lives of people through ease of use items. These innovations can do things automatically to spare some time off of users to do other things. However, these can also be harmful by making people too reliant on these innovations and forget how to do simple things without them.

Self driving cars can be beneficial through things like getting a ride from someone or delivering something. These things will take the ease off of drivers for companies and make them more efficient. Robots that do tasks also do this, it will make the lives of people like cargo workers easier because the robots will do the heavy lifting and keep the physical labor to the machines and not the people.
